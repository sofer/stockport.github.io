Local Help

Feeling suicidal is a very difficult place to be as It can be very emotional and distressing. We want to help you stay safe and feel better. Below is some advice and help including information on where to find support if you are in crisis. If you are having suicidal thoughts it may feel you don’t have anyone to talk to but there are people who want to listen and support you. They can’t help you unless they know how you feel.

What they provide
Contact
Samaritans
Confidential listening service for people 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Talk to us any time you like, in your own way, and off the record – about whatever’s getting to you.
Ring 0161 432 1221 or 08457 909090 or Email jo@samaritans.org
Talk to your own GP who can assess you, provide support and refer you on to other services.
Stockport Psychological Wellbeing Service
Stockport Psychological Wellbeing Service provides a range of effective, evidence based help and support to anyone with a GP based in Stockport aged 11 years and over. Our wait time is usually no longer than 2 weeks and services include one-one CBT based support, online therapies and structured groups.
Please call 01614802020 or visit our website www.stockportpws.org.uk for more information and to refer yourself.
Beacon Counselling
Confidential counselling service for people over 18 covering depression, anxiety, stress, bereavement and loss, relationships, family breakdown, trauma, childhood abuse, domestic violence and abuse.
Ring for appointments 0161 440 0055 or email office@beacon-counselling.org.uk
Self help services
Support through our counselling and Cognitive Behavioural Therapies (CBT) covering depression, anxiety, low self-esteem, low mood, Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD) and panic disorders.
The Sanctuary: ‘overnight, every night’ service. Ring for an appointment on 0161 637 0808 or ask GP to refer. www.selfhelpservices.org.uk
The Women's Centre
Counselling and Psychotherapy Service and a range of Services including 1:1 case work, drop-ins and group work. Supporting women experiencing stress, anxiety, depression, domestic abuse, childhood abuse, loss, relationships difficulties, trauma and psychological distress.
Contact 0161 355 4455, email admin@stockportwomenscentre.co.uk, www.thewomenscentre.uk.net
Stockport Mind
Drop in services or support with information on mental health services.
The Sir Joseph Whitworth Centre, Chestergate 0161 480 7393
Stockport Progress and Recovery Centre
Support to adults who experience mental health problems.
